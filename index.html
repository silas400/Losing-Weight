Calorie Burn Basics

Most calories are burned just staying alive.
This refers to your basal metabolic rate (BMR), which is the number of calories your body needs to function at rest. These functions include breathing, circulating blood, and maintaining body temperature. It’s surprising to many, but the majority of your daily energy is spent just keeping you alive. That’s why understanding your BMR is crucial for managing weight.

Food and Calorie Management

Digesting food burns calories—some more than others.
Certain foods can naturally boost your metabolism because they require more energy to digest—a concept known as the thermic effect of food. Protein-rich items like eggs, chicken, and Greek yogurt burn the most calories during digestion. High-fiber vegetables such as broccoli and spinach, and whole grains like oats and brown rice, also demand more effort to break down. Spicy foods containing capsaicin, like chili peppers, can slightly raise energy expenditure, while drinks like green tea and coffee provide compounds that gently stimulate metabolism. Choosing these foods can help your body burn more calories with each meal.
